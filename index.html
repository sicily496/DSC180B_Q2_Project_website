Introduction

Deep neural networks (DNNs) have become the cornerstone of modern machine learning, but often demand extensive computational resources. To address this gap, model compression techniques have emerged as a vital area of research, aiming to reduce computational and storage overhead while preserving accuracy. Existing methods, such as quantization (reducing numerical precision of weights) and knowledge distillation (transferring knowledge from a large ”teacher” model to a compact ”student” model), have shown promise individually. However, the interplay between these techniques—particularly their combined impact on accuracy, storage efficiency, and inference speed—remains underexplored. In this project, we propose a hybrid compression strategy that systematically integrates knowledge distillation and post-training quantization to optimize the trade-off between model efficiency and performance.

Method

We initially began testing with a classical knowledge distillation method, where we worked to distill a ResNet50 model into a ResNet18 model. From this classical model, we each implemented a unique method that modifies the existing algorithm to improve overall efficiency

KD Strategies

  • Mixup:

    Generated interpolated training samples via linear blending of image-label pairs (Beta distribution) to enhance feature diversity and decision boundary smoothness.

  • Vanilla Knowledge Distillation:

    Achieved 91.52% accuracy using cross-entropy loss for logit alignment between ResNet50 teacher and ResNet18 student.

  • Deep Mutual Learning:

    Co-trained dual ResNet18 students (default vs pretrained initialization) with QAT-layer fusion, achieving mutual performance boost through 200-epoch interaction.

  • Decoupled Knowledge Distillation:

    Separated loss into target-class (TCKD) and non-target-class (NCKD) components, optimizing via α=2/β=10 parameters for 90.64% accuracy.

Post-Training Quantization

  • GPFQ (Greedy Post-Training Feature Quantization):

    Applied greedy layer-wise quantization guided by accuracy impact analysis, compressing model while maintaining performance.

Experiment Results

Our experiments demonstrate that the distilled ResNet18 student maintains superior accuracy under aggressive quantization compared to the baseline ResNet18. At 8-bit precision, the distilled model achieves 89.7% accuracy (vs. 85.2% for vanilla ResNet18), preserving 98% of its original performance. Notably, even when compressed to 4-bit (0.3MB storage), the distilled model retains 82.4% accuracy - a 6.3% absolute improvement over the non-distilled counterpart at equivalent bit-width. This resilience validates that knowledge distillation creates quantization-friendly representations, where the student’s learned feature hierarchy withstands precision reduction better than standard-trained models.
